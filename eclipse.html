Solar Eclipses | What are they?

A solar eclipse is a type of eclipse that occurs when the Moon passes between the Sun and Earth, and when the moon fully or partially blocks the Sun. The solar eclipse creates a falling shadow onto the Earth while the Moon blocks the Sun.



To be a total solar eclipse, the Moon must be in front of the Sun and have the orbital planes All of the planets, comets, and asteroids in the solar system are in orbit around the Sun. All of those orbits line up with each other making a kind of flat disk called the orbital plane. intersecting.



When and Where?

The next total solar eclipse will occur on August 21 in certain locations exclusive to the United States. The most optimal place to watch the eclipse with a duration of 2 minutes and 41 seconds would be in the tiny town of Carbondale, Illinois. According to the article "Solar eclipse 2017: What you need to know" by BBC, "The total eclipse is scheduled to begin at 10:16 (14:16 GMT) in Government Point, Oregon, travelling across Idaho, Wyoming, Nebraska, North Carolina, Georgia and South Carolina, where it will end at 14:48 (18:48 GMT)."

Safety

Safety is important, especially when looking at the sun for long periods of times, so here are ways you can prepare for the upcoming eclipse and how you can view it safely:

  1. Solar Sunglasses
  2. Make a projector
  3. Telescopes with Solar Filters


Make sure that you only look at the sun if you use one of these tools properly. The consequences of looking at the sun without these tools are costly.
